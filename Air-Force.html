Title: Air Force
Year: 2023
Size: ​20x30 inch
Media: ​Mixed Material

When we turn on the media today, most news is about wars, all the bad news. So this collection is about wars but also hope.

This collection has 3 paintings, each representing a soldier from a different unit.

They all have the same thoughts in their mind: When is the war going to end? When can I go home and see my family? What have I done?

I didn’t do abstract that much, but this time, I want to use the abstract style to express the chaos of the war and the struggle of the soldier’s mind. At the same time, I want audiences to recognize a human form easily, so I have done some study in anatomy.

When you turn off the light or use a black light, some parts of the painting will glow in the dark. The concept behind this is to remind audiences that even if we live in the dark, there will always be light and hope that guides us.

“War. War never changes. Since the dawn of humankind, when our ancestors first discovered the killing power of rock and bone, blood has been spilled in the name of everything: from God to justice to simple, psychotic rage.” - Fallout Intro 

Bring to the table win-win survival strategies to ensure proactive domination. At the end of the day, going forward, a new normal that has evolved from generation X is on the runway heading towards a streamlined cloud solution.

About the artist

Wei Ho a.k.a Jacky Ho

An artist born and raised in Taiwan, who has been influenced by his American sci-fi movies and TV shows since age 5, his parents always wanted him to become a teacher, lawyer, or doctor, you name it. But this kid never followed the rules, sometimes giving his parents and teachers a headache. Years later, he became a fashion designer and teaches at universities and colleges. At least he came with a textile engineer background; he is still an engineer, right? Remember, as did his designs and artworks, he never followed the rules. He likes to combine a lot of different mediums with some science—his painting combined with fabric, coﬀee ground, 3D printing technology…etc. Most importantly, most of his artwork comes as a collection with a story behind it, making it more interesting.
